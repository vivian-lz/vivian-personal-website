why am i here?

i like to think i always wanted to be an engineer. i have always loved mathematics since i was little for the simple fact that you can take problems and think really hard until you craft a correct solution that is really rewarding, which engineers do on an innovative scale. anyone who reads a "why engineering" essay from me would know that when i was 11 i watched youtube videos of child prodigy innovators that inspired me to pursue engineering, simply because of the fact that i loved how they could take trash and truly turn it to treasure, tinkering and playing with electronic parts to create a product that can actually help the communities around them. that spark of excitement to tinker with electronics will always be my north star as to why i do, well, what i do.

i am an electrical and comupter engineering major at vanderbilt university with minors in math and digital fabrication (probably, those minors are not set in stone). i am currently only an incoming sophomore as i am making this, but i have done a few engineering projects.
